\begin{center}
  {\large\bfseries RetroDiff: Training Objective Derivation}
\end{center}

\medskip

\noindent
Let $x$ denote a clean data sample and $x_t$ its noised counterpart at diffusion step $t \in \{1,\ldots,T\}$.
The forward process $q(x_{1:T}|x)$ gradually corrupts $x$; the model $p_\theta$ learns to reverse it.
Applying Jensen's inequality to the log-likelihood yields the \emph{evidence lower bound} (ELBO):

\begin{align}
  \log p_\theta(x)
    &\;\geq\;
    \mathbb{E}_{q(x_{1:T}|x)}\!\left[\log\frac{p_\theta(x_{0:T})}{q(x_{1:T}|x)}\right]
    \;=\; -\mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{ELBO}}
  \label{eq:elbo}
\end{align}

\noindent
Because the Markov structure of the forward process allows the joint to factorise,
the bound in \eqref{eq:elbo} decomposes into a reconstruction term at $t{=}1$
and a sum of per-step KL divergences that penalise each denoising transition:

\begin{align}
  \mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{ELBO}}
    &\;=\;
    \underbrace{\mathbb{E}_q[-\log p_\theta(x_0|x_1)]}_{\text{reconstruction}}
    \;+\;
    \sum_{t=2}^{T}
    \underbrace{
      \mathrm{KL}\!\left(q(x_{t-1}|x_t,x)\,\|\,p_\theta(x_{t-1}|x_t)\right)
    }_{\text{step-}t\text{ denoising}}
  \label{eq:decomp}
\end{align}

\noindent
RetroDiff's key contribution is to condition every denoising step on a set
$R(x) = \{r_1,\ldots,r_k\}$ of exemplars retrieved from the training corpus
at inference time.
Substituting the conditioned ELBO from \eqref{eq:decomp} into an expectation
over the data distribution gives the final per-parameter objective:

\begin{align}
  \mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{RetroDiff}}(\theta)
    &\;=\;
    \mathbb{E}_{x\sim p_{\mathrm{data}}}\!\left[
      \mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{ELBO}}\!\left(x\,\big|\,R(x)\right)
    \right]
  \label{eq:retro}
\end{align}

\noindent
Minimising \eqref{eq:retro} over $\theta$ trains the reverse process to exploit
retrieved neighbours, reducing the effective variance of each denoising step
without altering the ELBO's theoretical guarantees.
