# Fixture: overclaim-paragraph

**Type:** writing — Discussion paragraph that overclaims and lacks boundaries
**Use in:** R1.C nature-polishing, R2 research-paper-writing
**Purpose:** Test whether the skill (a) detects causal overclaims, (b) inserts
boundaries / limitations, (c) demotes "first" claims that aren't substantiated.

## Source paragraph (deliberately overclaimed)

> Our results clearly demonstrate that microbiome composition causes
> susceptibility to inflammatory bowel disease (IBD), and we are the first to
> show this. The 4.3-fold enrichment of *Faecalibacterium prausnitzii* in
> healthy controls compared to IBD patients (n=82) proves that this organism is
> protective. Our findings will fundamentally change how IBD is treated, since
> probiotics targeting *F. prausnitzii* should now become standard of care. We
> also note that several other taxa were differentially abundant, but these are
> beyond the scope of the present discussion. The mechanism, while not yet
> elucidated, is almost certainly through short-chain fatty acid signalling,
> consistent with the broader literature.

## Brief for the runner

> Tighten this Discussion paragraph for a Nature-family gastroenterology
> manuscript. Maintain the scientific claim where supported; soften or remove
> where the data don't support the claim.

## Failure modes the runner should fix

- "Causes" — observational data don't establish causation
- "We are the first to show this" — needs a literature search to substantiate; safer to omit
- "4.3-fold enrichment ... proves that this organism is protective" — association is not protection
- "Will fundamentally change how IBD is treated" — overclaim; future-tense outcome stated as certainty
- "Should now become standard of care" — clinical recommendation from a single observational study
- "Almost certainly through short-chain fatty acid signalling" — speculation without mechanistic evidence
- No boundary or limitation acknowledged

## Reference rewrite expectations

1. Replace `causes / proves` with `is associated with / consistent with`
2. Drop `we are the first` unless supported
3. Replace `will fundamentally change / should now become standard of care`
   with bounded language about therapeutic implications and what would be needed
   to test them
4. Hedge mechanism speculation: `may / could be mediated by ... ; mechanistic
   tests are required`
5. Add at least one explicit limitation sentence (cohort size, single-site,
   association-only, no germ-free or transfer experiments)
