## Polished

Genome editing in the central nervous system could support studies and treatments for neurological disease, but delivery remains a major barrier. Viral vectors can be limited by immunogenicity, and systemic delivery must overcome the blood-brain barrier. We developed a lipid nanoparticle formulation containing an ionisable lipid, compound 14B, and a brain-targeting peptide to deliver CRISPR-Cas9 components to mouse brain after intravenous injection. The formulation delivered Cas9 mRNA and a guide RNA targeting *PCSK9*, producing editing in 38% of cortical neurons after 14 days and showing good tolerability. These findings identify a non-viral delivery strategy for brain genome editing and support further evaluation in neurological disease models.
