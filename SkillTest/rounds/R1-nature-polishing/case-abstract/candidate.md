## Polished

Precise genome editing in the central nervous system could clarify disease mechanisms and support targeted therapeutic strategies. Delivery remains difficult because systemic agents must cross the blood-brain barrier, whereas viral vectors can raise immunogenicity concerns. Here, we show that a lipid nanoparticle containing ionisable lipid 14B and a brain-targeting peptide delivers CRISPR-Cas9 cargo to mouse brain after intravenous injection. Delivery of Cas9 mRNA and a guide RNA targeting *PCSK9* edited 38% of cortical neurons at 14 days and was well tolerated. These findings establish a non-viral route for CNS genome editing in mice and define a platform for further preclinical evaluation.
