## Polished

Our results associate microbiome composition with inflammatory bowel disease (IBD) status, but they do not establish causation. In this cohort, *Faecalibacterium prausnitzii* was enriched 4.3-fold in healthy controls compared with IBD patients (n = 82). This pattern is consistent with a protective association, rather than proof of protection. The findings support further investigation of *F. prausnitzii*-targeted probiotics, including prospective and mechanistic studies. Because the study was observational and did not include transfer experiments, standard-of-care recommendations would be premature. Short-chain fatty acid signalling may contribute, but this mechanism remains to be tested directly.
