## Polished

Our results show an association between microbiome composition and susceptibility to inflammatory bowel disease (IBD). In this cohort, *Faecalibacterium prausnitzii* was enriched 4.3-fold in healthy controls compared with IBD patients (n = 82), consistent with a potential protective association. These findings may inform future studies of microbiome-based interventions, but they do not support a clinical recommendation at this stage. Several other taxa were also differentially abundant and warrant further analysis. Short-chain fatty acid signalling provides a plausible mechanistic link, although direct mechanistic experiments will be needed to test this possibility.
