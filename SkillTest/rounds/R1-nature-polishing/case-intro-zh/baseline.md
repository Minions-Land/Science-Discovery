## Polished

Protein language models (PLMs) have recently been applied across many biological tasks, including structure prediction, functional annotation and mutation-effect prediction. Despite these gains, current PLM training paradigms rely heavily on large sequence datasets that often show species bias and imbalanced functional annotation. These biases can limit generalisation to rare protein families and under-characterised functional classes. In this study, we examine this problem and introduce a training framework designed to improve PLM robustness for long-tail protein families. Experiments on six benchmarks show that the method outperforms existing baselines.
