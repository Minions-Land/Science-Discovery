## Polished

Treated cells showed a 3.2-fold increase in target-gene expression compared with controls (Fig. 2c; n = 4 per group; two-tailed t-test, p = 0.011). We then performed single-cell RNA sequencing on the same population and identified a rare subset, representing approximately 4% of cells, that expressed both the target gene and a known co-activator. This subset was enriched in the treated condition (1.8-fold; hypergeometric p = 0.003). Finally, co-activator knockdown abolished the response, supporting a requirement for this factor in the observed expression change.
