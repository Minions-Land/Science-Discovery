## Polished

Recent advances in single-cell RNA-seq enable characterization of rare cell populations at high resolution. Vento-Tormo et al. (2018) profiled the maternal-fetal interface and identified a previously uncharacterized decidual-NK subset, revealing that immune cell heterogeneity in this niche substantially exceeds what bulk transcriptomics suggested. Building on these foundations, we characterized a similar subset in the spleen, where conventional dendritic cells exhibit a transcriptional signature associated with chronic infection. We sequenced 12,400 splenic dendritic cells from C57BL/6 mice across three infection timepoints. Differential expression analysis identified 47 genes upregulated specifically in the late-infection cluster, including markers of metabolic stress. High-resolution profiling resolves immune cell adaptation during infection.
