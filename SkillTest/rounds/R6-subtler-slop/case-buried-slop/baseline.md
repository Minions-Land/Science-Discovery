## Polished

Recent advances in single-cell RNA-seq have enabled high-resolution characterization of rare immune populations. Vento-Tormo et al. (2018) profiled the maternal-fetal interface and identified a previously uncharacterized decidual NK subset, revealing immune cell heterogeneity far richer than bulk transcriptomics suggested. Building on these foundations, we applied similar high-resolution profiling to characterize conventional dendritic cells in the spleen during chronic infection. We sequenced 12,400 splenic dendritic cells from C57BL/6 mice across three infection timepoints. Differential expression analysis identified 47 genes upregulated specifically in the late-infection cluster, including markers of metabolic stress. These findings demonstrate that single-cell profiling is essential for understanding immune cell adaptation during persistent infection.
