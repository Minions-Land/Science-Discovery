## Polished

Single-cell RNA-seq has enabled characterization of rare immune populations at high resolution. Vento-Tormo et al. (2018) profiled the maternal-fetal interface and identified a previously uncharacterized decidual-NK subset, revealing that immune cell heterogeneity in this niche exceeds what bulk transcriptomics suggested. We applied similar high-resolution mapping to the spleen, where conventional dendritic cells display a distinct transcriptional signature associated with chronic infection. We sequenced 12,400 splenic dendritic cells from C57BL/6 mice across three infection timepoints. Differential expression analysis identified 47 genes upregulated specifically in the late-infection cluster, including markers of metabolic stress. This subset exhibits a transcriptional program distinct from steady-state dendritic cells. High-resolution profiling thus reveals cell states that escape detection in bulk analysis and provides a foundation for mechanistic studies of immune adaptation during persistent infection.
