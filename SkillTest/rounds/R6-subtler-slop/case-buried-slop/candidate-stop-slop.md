## Polished

Recent advances in single-cell RNA-seq enable characterization of rare cell populations at high resolution. Vento-Tormo et al. (2018) profiled the maternal-fetal interface and identified a previously uncharacterized decidual-NK subset, revealing that immune cell heterogeneity in this niche exceeds what bulk transcriptomics suggested. We applied similar high-resolution mapping to characterize a dendritic cell subset in the spleen. We sequenced 12,400 splenic dendritic cells from C57BL/6 mice across three infection timepoints. Differential expression analysis identified 47 genes upregulated specifically in the late-infection cluster, including markers of metabolic stress. These genes suggest a role for conventional dendritic cells in chronic infection responses. High-resolution profiling thus reveals immune cell adaptation mechanisms that bulk methods obscure.
