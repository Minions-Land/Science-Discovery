# Response to Reviewers

Dear Editor,

We have addressed each reviewer concern below with a mapped action and manuscript destination. Page and line numbers are marked as placeholders until the revised manuscript is paginated.

**R1.C1 — ACCEPT_EXPERIMENT / ACCEPT_ANALYSIS.** The reviewer requested replication in a second mouse strain with n>=12 and neuron-subtype stratification. We agree that this is needed to support the generality claim. We will add a second-strain cohort (n=12 per group) and report editing efficiency separately for excitatory and inhibitory neurons. These results will be added to Results, Fig. 2, Supplementary Table S1, and Methods (page [X], lines [Y-Z]).

**R1.C2 — ACCEPT_FIGURE + ACCEPT_TEXT / PARTIAL.** The reviewer noted that the 30-45% y-axis in Fig. 2c may exaggerate regional differences. We agree that the main figure should show the full bounded scale and will replot Fig. 2c on a 0-100% axis. We will also explain in the Fig. 2 legend that the original truncated view was used to inspect within-range regional variation under FDA-style graphical guidance, and, if retained, place that version in Supplementary Fig. S2.

**R1.C3 — DISAGREE_WITH_JUSTIFICATION + SOFTEN_CLAIM.** The reviewer argued that the approximately 22% cholesterol reduction is modest relative to statins and that the clinical relevance claim should be removed. We respectfully disagree that the claim should be removed entirely, because the manuscript does not position CNS-only PCSK9 editing as magnitude-equivalent to systemic statin therapy. The scientific relevance is the mechanistically distinct CNS-restricted PCSK9 perturbation producing a measurable plasma cholesterol effect. To avoid overstatement, we will revise the Discussion (page [X], lines [Y-Z]) to state this as mechanistic relevance rather than direct clinical competitiveness.

**R2.C1 — ACCEPT_TEXT.** The reviewer requested chemical characterisation of compound 14B. We agree. We will add 1H-NMR, mass spectrometry, and HPLC purity data to Supplementary Fig. S3 and describe the analytical methods in Supplementary Methods (page [X], lines [Y-Z]).

**R2.C2 — ACCEPT_EXPERIMENT + SOFTEN_CLAIM.** The reviewer requested a peptide-free control for the BBB-crossing mechanism claim. We agree. We will add a peptide-free LNP comparator and report its brain delivery/editing results against the targeting-peptide formulation in Fig. 2 and Supplementary Fig. S4. Until that control is shown, we will revise the Abstract and Discussion to frame BBB crossing as supported by the peptide-comparison experiment rather than inferred from the targeting design alone.
