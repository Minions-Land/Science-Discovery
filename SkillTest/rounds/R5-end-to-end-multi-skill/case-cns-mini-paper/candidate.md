## Abstract

CRISPR-Cas9 genome editing in the central nervous system is limited by inefficient non-viral delivery across the blood-brain barrier. Here, we introduce a lipid nanoparticle (LNP) formulation that combines the ionisable lipid compound 14B with a brain-targeting peptide for systemic delivery of Cas9 mRNA and a guide RNA targeting *PCSK9*. Microfluidic mixing at a 3:1 flow ratio produced 78-nm particles with a PDI of 0.12 and 92% encapsulation efficiency. After intravenous administration in mice, the formulation yielded 38% editing in cortical neurons at day 14 and was well tolerated in the tested cohort. These results establish a non-viral route for CNS genome editing in mice and support further preclinical evaluation of compound 14B LNPs.

## Discussion paragraph

Microbiome composition was associated with IBD status in this cohort. *Faecalibacterium prausnitzii* was enriched 4.3-fold in healthy controls relative to patients with IBD (n=82), consistent with an association between this taxon and health. The study does not include bacterial transfer experiments to germ-free recipients, strain-level intervention, or prospective supplementation; therefore, it cannot establish that *F. prausnitzii* is protective or justify clinical adoption of probiotics. These findings support mechanistic testing of this association before therapeutic claims are made.
