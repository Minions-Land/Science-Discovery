Our method achieves 0.881 mAP and 0.89 F1, outperforming the strong DiffSeg-XL baseline (0.847 mAP, 0.87 F1) while reducing latency from 412 ms to 96 ms. The key to this improvement is the topology-aware refinement head (Module Z), which operates on segmentation logits to enforce spatial consistency. The ablation row confirms Z's importance: removing it drops mAP to 0.853 and F1 to 0.86, a loss of 0.028 and 0.03 respectively, while saving only 8 ms of latency. This trade-off demonstrates that Z's refinement is essential for accuracy. DiffSeg-XL's high latency stems from its 4-step diffusion process at inference; our approach avoids this bottleneck entirely, achieving both higher accuracy and 4× faster inference.
