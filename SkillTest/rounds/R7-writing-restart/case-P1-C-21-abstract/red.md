Cell segmentation in fluorescence microscopy remains a critical bottleneck for quantitative biology. While supervised methods like U-Net and Cellpose are widely used, they require ~10,000 annotated images per cell type, limiting their applicability to new microscopy modalities. Here, we introduce ContrastiveCellSeg, a self-supervised pretraining approach that substantially reduces annotation requirements. We trained a ResNet-50 backbone using SimCLR-style contrastive learning on 2.3 million unlabeled patches from public datasets, then fine-tuned on as few as 100 labeled cells. On the LIVECell test set, ContrastiveCellSeg achieves a mean average precision of 0.847 at IoU 0.5, representing a 16% relative improvement over U-Net (0.731) and outperforming fine-tuned Cellpose (0.802). This demonstrates that contrastive pretraining can substantially reduce the annotation burden for fluorescence-microscopy segmentation, with broad applications across cell biology, drug discovery, and clinical pathology.
