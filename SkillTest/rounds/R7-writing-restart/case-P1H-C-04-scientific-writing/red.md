Tissue clearing is essential for whole-organ imaging, yet existing protocols remain limited in imaging depth. Current methods such as CLARITY and iDISCO achieve imaging depths of approximately 3–4 mm in adult mouse brain, which is insufficient for primate and human samples. Here we introduce Hydrolipid-Permeation Clearing (HPC), a single-step protocol combining a charged surfactant cocktail with a refractive-index-matched immersion medium that substantially improves imaging penetration. HPC processes a 5 mm mouse brain hemisphere in 9 days—faster than CLARITY (14–21 days)—while preserving endogenous fluorescent-protein signal with minimal intensity loss (8.3% versus 31.7% for CLARITY). On adult mouse brain, HPC achieves a 12-fold increase in imaging depth compared to CLARITY, reaching 36 mm in a single light-sheet acquisition. This advancement enables deep imaging of large mammalian organs and is compatible with standard fluorescent probes and confocal microscopes, offering a practical solution for whole-organ visualization in complex biological systems.
