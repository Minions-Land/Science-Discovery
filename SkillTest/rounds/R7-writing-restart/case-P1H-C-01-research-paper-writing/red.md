Tissue clearing enables high-resolution imaging of intact organs, but current methods are limited by imaging depth. CLARITY and iDISCO achieve depths of ~3–4 mm in adult mouse brain, insufficient for primate and human samples. Here we introduce Hydrolipid-Permeation Clearing (HPC), a single-step protocol combining a charged surfactant cocktail (4% SDS + 0.5% Triton X-100) with a refractive-index-matched immersion medium (n = 1.466). HPC processes a 5 mm mouse brain hemisphere in 9 days—40% faster than CLARITY—while preserving endogenous fluorescent-protein signal with only 8.3% intensity loss, compared to 31.7% for CLARITY. On adult mouse brain, HPC achieves a 12-fold increase in imaging depth, reaching 36 mm in a single light-sheet acquisition. The method is compatible with standard fluorescent probes and confocal microscopes, enabling deep imaging of large mammalian organs without specialized equipment. HPC substantially expands the scale and accessibility of whole-organ imaging.
