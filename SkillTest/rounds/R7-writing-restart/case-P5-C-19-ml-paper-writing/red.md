Our method achieves 0.881 mAP and 0.89 F1, substantially outperforming the 2024 SOTA baseline (DiffSeg-XL: 0.847 mAP, 0.87 F1) while maintaining practical latency (96 ms vs. 412 ms). DiffSeg-XL's strength on mAP reflects the power of diffusion-based refinement, but its four-step inference process becomes prohibitive for real-time applications. Our approach replaces this with a topology-aware refinement head (Module Z) that operates directly on segmentation logits, achieving comparable accuracy gains with 8× lower latency. The ablation (no Module Z: 0.853 mAP, 0.86 F1) confirms that Z is the load-bearing component, contributing ~2.8% mAP improvement while adding only 8 ms. The 1990 baseline (0.412 mAP) provides historical context and demonstrates the progress in the field over three decades.
