Cell segmentation in fluorescence microscopy remains a bottleneck for quantitative biology, largely because supervised methods such as U-Net and Cellpose require ~10,000 annotated images per cell type—impractical for new microscopy modalities. Here we introduce ContrastiveCellSeg, a self-supervised pretraining approach that substantially reduces annotation burden. We apply SimCLR-style contrastive learning to 2.3 million unlabeled patches from LIVECell and CellPose-Cyto2 datasets using a ResNet-50 backbone, then fine-tune on as few as 100 labeled cells. On the LIVECell test split, ContrastiveCellSeg achieves a mean average precision of 0.847 at IoU threshold 0.5—a 16% relative improvement over U-Net (0.731) and outperforming fine-tuned Cellpose (0.802). This work demonstrates that contrastive pretraining can substantially reduce the annotation cost of fluorescence-microscopy segmentation, with broad applications in cell biology, drug discovery, and clinical pathology.
