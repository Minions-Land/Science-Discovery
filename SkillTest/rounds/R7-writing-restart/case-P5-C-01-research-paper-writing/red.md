Our method achieves state-of-the-art results while maintaining practical inference speed. We attain 0.881 mAP and 0.89 F1, surpassing the strong DiffSeg-XL baseline (0.847 mAP, 0.87 F1) through a topology-aware refinement head that operates on segmentation logits. This design choice directly improves boundary precision without requiring expensive diffusion steps; DiffSeg-XL's 412 ms latency reflects its 4-step diffusion process at inference, whereas our method completes in 96 ms. The ablation row (no module Z) demonstrates that the refinement head is the load-bearing component: removing it drops mAP to 0.853 and F1 to 0.86, while adding only ~8 ms latency. We include the 1990 template-matching baseline for historical context, showing the substantial progress in the field over three decades.
