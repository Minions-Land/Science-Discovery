Cell segmentation in fluorescence microscopy remains a critical bottleneck for quantitative biology. While supervised methods such as U-Net and Cellpose are widely adopted, they require ~10,000 annotated images per cell type, limiting their applicability to new microscopy modalities. Here we introduce ContrastiveCellSeg, a self-supervised pretraining approach that substantially reduces annotation requirements. We apply SimCLR-style contrastive learning to 2.3 million unlabeled patches from LIVECell and CellPose-Cyto2 datasets using a ResNet-50 backbone, then fine-tune on as few as 100 labeled cells. On the held-out LIVECell test set, ContrastiveCellSeg achieves mean AP of 0.847 (IoU ≥ 0.5), representing a 16% relative improvement over U-Net (0.731) and 5.6% over fine-tuned Cellpose (0.802). These results demonstrate that contrastive pretraining can substantially reduce the annotation burden for fluorescence-microscopy segmentation, with broad applications across cell biology, drug discovery, and clinical pathology.
