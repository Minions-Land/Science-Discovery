Cell segmentation in fluorescence microscopy remains a critical bottleneck for quantitative biology. While supervised methods such as U-Net and Cellpose are widely used, they require approximately 10,000 annotated images per cell type, making them impractical for new microscopy modalities. Here we introduce ContrastiveCellSeg, a self-supervised pretraining approach that substantially reduces annotation requirements. We trained a ResNet-50 backbone using SimCLR-style contrastive learning on 2.3 million unlabeled patches from the LIVECell and CellPose-Cyto2 datasets, followed by fine-tuning on as few as 100 labeled cells. On the held-out LIVECell test split, ContrastiveCellSeg achieved a mean average precision of 0.847 at IoU threshold 0.5, representing a 16% relative improvement over U-Net (0.731) and outperforming fine-tuned Cellpose (0.802). These results demonstrate that contrastive pretraining can substantially reduce the annotation burden for fluorescence-microscopy segmentation, with broad applications across cell biology, drug discovery, and clinical pathology.
