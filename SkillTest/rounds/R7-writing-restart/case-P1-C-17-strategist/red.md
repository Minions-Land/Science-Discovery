Cell segmentation in fluorescence microscopy remains a critical bottleneck for quantitative biology. While supervised methods such as U-Net and Cellpose are widely adopted, they require ~10,000 annotated images per cell type, which is impractical for emerging microscopy modalities. Here we introduce ContrastiveCellSeg, a self-supervised pretraining approach that substantially reduces annotation requirements. We applied SimCLR-style contrastive learning to 2.3 million unlabeled patches from LIVECell and CellPose-Cyto2 datasets, using a ResNet-50 backbone with a 2-layer projection head trained for 200 epochs on 8× A100 GPUs. Fine-tuning on as few as 100 labeled cells achieves a mean average precision of 0.847 at IoU threshold 0.5 on the held-out LIVECell test split—a 16% relative improvement over U-Net (0.731) and outperforming fine-tuned Cellpose (0.802). This work demonstrates that contrastive pretraining can dramatically reduce the annotation burden for fluorescence-microscopy segmentation, with broad applications across cell biology, drug discovery, and clinical pathology.
