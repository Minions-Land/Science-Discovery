Cell segmentation in fluorescence microscopy remains a critical bottleneck for quantitative biology. Supervised methods such as U-Net and Cellpose are widely adopted, but require ~10,000 annotated images per cell type, making them impractical for new microscopy modalities. Here we introduce ContrastiveCellSeg, a self-supervised pretraining approach that substantially reduces annotation requirements. We apply SimCLR-style contrastive learning to 2.3 million unlabeled patches from LIVECell and CellPose-Cyto2 datasets using a ResNet-50 backbone, then fine-tune on as few as 100 labeled cells. On the held-out LIVECell test set, ContrastiveCellSeg achieves mean average precision of 0.847 (IoU ≥ 0.5), compared to 0.731 for U-Net and 0.802 for fine-tuned Cellpose—a 16% relative improvement over U-Net. Our results demonstrate that contrastive pretraining can dramatically lower the annotation burden for fluorescence-microscopy segmentation, with broad applications across cell biology, drug discovery, and clinical pathology.
